For reviewers

This page provides a concise and verifiable view of the research scope, evaluation protocol, cost model assumptions, and governance stance. No sensitive operational data is disclosed. Public artefacts are synthetic or anonymised.

Evaluation protocol PDF Cost model note PDF Model card template PDF

Problem and contribution

  • Acquiring fraud detection is non stationary. Random splits inflate performance and hide drift sensitivity.
  • False positives create friction and operational cost. False negatives create direct loss. Accuracy is not the objective.
  • This research proposes a time aware evaluation protocol plus a cost sensitive decision framework suitable for regulated environments.

Intended outcome. Better net benefit and better governance under drift, not just higher offline scores.

Scope

  • Payments. Merchant acquiring.
  • Targets. False positives and false negatives.
  • Evaluation. Rolling windows and out of time holdout.
  • Governance. Auditability and human oversight for high impact decisions.

Method overview

  • Blocked time series validation and rolling window backtests.
  • Explicit leakage control via feature catalog and time consistent aggregations.
  • Cost curves and threshold policies constrained by operational capacity.
  • Governance artefacts. Model card, data disclosure, release gates, drift monitoring.

Limitations

  • Public artefacts rely on synthetic data. Production data remains confidential.
  • Cost assumptions are scenario based. Sensitivity analysis is mandatory.
  • Results must be interpreted under drift and operational constraints.

Reproducibility stance

  • Protocol and templates are public.
  • Planned open source. Synthetic generator and evaluation harness.
  • Experiment snapshots will be shared as publishable, non sensitive summaries.
